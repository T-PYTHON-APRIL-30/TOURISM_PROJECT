Madain Saleh is an archaeological site located in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. The cultural history of Madain Saleh dates back to the period when the Nabataean state flourished in the first centuries BC and the first AD before its fall at the hands of the Roman Empire in 106 AD. Madain Saleh includes 153 carved rock facades in addition to many of Islamic monuments.
